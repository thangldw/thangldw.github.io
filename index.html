About

I'm a data-driven problem solver who works at the seam where engineering and business meet. Over the past decade, I've moved from statistical analysis to data engineering, and now to leading technical delivery as a Front Technical Lead at FPT Japan.

I still see myself as a student of this craft. I learn most from the people I build with, especially when the work is uncertain, the data is messy, and the right answer is not obvious yet.

My best work often starts with ambiguity: a vague business goal, a complex dataset, or a customer who feels the pain but has not yet found the shape of the solution. I try to get close to the problem, understand what the data is really saying, and find the simpler path that creates real leverage.

From there, I help turn ideas into working systems: consulting on architecture, building pipelines and models, leading delivery teams of 30–50 engineers including offshore members, and supporting the pre-sales, negotiation, and stakeholder work needed to keep people aligned.

I care about building systems that are useful, reliable, and transferable — not just something that works once, but something the next engineer and the customer team can understand, operate, and improve.

How I work

Embed & discover

I try to start where the problem actually lives — with the users, the data, and the stakeholders. My work in solution consulting and pre-sales has taught me to slow down early, listen carefully, and reframe the ask until the real constraint becomes clear, before writing the first line of code.

Build & ship

I care about building systems that can survive production, not just demos: reliable ELT pipelines, recommendation and ranking models, and BI that leaders actually use. I've worked across cloud platforms and the full project lifecycle, trying to move fast without making the solution fragile.

Lead & enable

The real deliverable is not only code, but a team that can keep moving after handover. I lead teams of 30–50 engineers, including offshore members, and focus on transferring requirements, designs, and context clearly so people understand the system, own it, and extend it with confidence.
