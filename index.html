About

I'm a data-driven problem solver who works where engineering and business meet. Over the past decade I've moved from statistical analysis to data engineering, and now to leading technical delivery as a Front Technical Lead at a global IT consultancy in Tokyo. I still see myself as a student of this craft — I learn most from the people I build with.

My best work starts in ambiguity: a vague goal, a messy dataset, a customer who feels the pain but hasn't found the shape of the solution. I get close to the problem, understand what the data is really saying, and find the simpler path that creates real leverage.

From there I turn ideas into working systems — consulting on architecture, building pipelines and models, and leading delivery teams of 30–50 engineers (including offshore), alongside the pre-sales, negotiation, and stakeholder work that keeps people aligned. I care about systems that are useful, reliable, and transferable — not something that works once, but something the next engineer and the customer team can understand, operate, and improve.

How I work

Embed & discover

I start where the problem actually lives — with the users, the data, and the stakeholders. Years of solution consulting and pre-sales taught me to slow down early, listen carefully, and reframe the ask until the real constraint becomes clear, before writing the first line of code.

Build & ship

I build systems that survive production, not just demos: reliable ELT pipelines, recommendation and ranking models, and BI that leaders actually use. I've shipped across cloud platforms and the full project lifecycle, moving fast without making the solution fragile.

Lead & enable

The real deliverable is not only code, but a team that can keep moving after handover. I lead teams of 30–50 engineers, including offshore members, and focus on transferring requirements, designs, and context clearly so people understand the system, own it, and extend it with confidence.
